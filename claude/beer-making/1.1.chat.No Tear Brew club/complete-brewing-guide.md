# Complete Beer Brewing Guide: Grains, Recipes & Techniques

## Malting Barley Fundamentals

### Primary Malting Varieties

1. Copeland

- Industry standard 2-row variety
- Clean fermentation profile
- Protein content: 11.5-12.5%
- High diastatic power: 140-160°L
- Excellent modification potential
- Base cost: $0.85-1.00/lb bulk
- Ideal storage: 6 months at <13% moisture

2. Metcalfe

- Extract efficiency: 80-82%
- Superior modification
- Protein content: 11-13%
- Diastatic power: 130-150°L
- Craft brewing favorite
- Base cost: $0.90-1.10/lb bulk
- Consistent performance

3. CDC Bow

- Disease resistant modern variety
- Clean, neutral flavor profile
- Protein content: 11.5-12.8%
- Diastatic power: 135-155°L
- Good yield potential
- Base cost: $0.85-1.05/lb bulk
- Excellent uniformity

4. Full Pint

- Craft brewing specific
- Rich malty flavor
- Protein content: 12-13%
- Diastatic power: 120-140°L
- Superior flavor development
- Base cost: $1.00-1.20/lb bulk
- Best for malt-forward styles

### Specialty Grains

1. Flaked Barley

- Pre-gelatinized form
- Protein content: ~7%
- No enzymatic activity
- Usage rate: 5-15% maximum
- Cost: $2-3/lb retail
- Adds: Body, mouthfeel, head retention
- Storage: 12 months sealed

2. Crystal/Caramel Malts

- Available in 10-120°L
- Used for color and flavor
- Usage rate: 2-15%
- Cost: $2-4/lb
- Adds: Sweetness, color, complexity
- No conversion needed

3. Roasted Malts

- Types: Chocolate, Black, Roasted Barley
- Color range: 200-600°L
- Usage rate: 1-10%
- Cost: $3-5/lb
- Adds: Dark colors, roasted flavors
- Use sparingly

## Standard Recipes

### Classic American Ale

Base Recipe:

- 9 lbs Copeland 2-row (or Metcalfe)
- 0.5 lb Crystal 60L
- 0.25 lb Victory Malt

Hops:

- 1 oz Hallertau (60 min)
- 0.5 oz East Kent Goldings (30 min)
- 0.5 oz Saaz (15 min)

Yeast:

- Nottingham Ale
- Ferment at 65-68°F
- Expected FG: 1.010-1.014
- ABV: 5.2-5.5%

### English Style Ale with Flaked Barley

Base Recipe:

- 8 lbs Maris Otter
- 1 lb Flaked Barley
- 0.5 lb Crystal 40L
- 0.25 lb Victory Malt

Hops:

- 1.5 oz East Kent Goldings (60 min)
- 0.5 oz Fuggle (15 min)

Yeast:

- London Ale III
- Ferment at 66-68°F
- Expected FG: 1.012-1.016
- ABV: 4.8-5.1%

### Irish Stout

Base Recipe:

- 8 lbs Pale Malt (Copeland or Metcalfe)
- 1 lb Flaked Barley
- 0.75 lb Roasted Barley
- 0.5 lb Crystal 60L

Hops:

- 2 oz East Kent Goldings (60 min)

Yeast:

- Irish Ale
- Ferment at 62-66°F
- Expected FG: 1.010-1.014
- ABV: 4.5-4.8%

## Brewing Process Guidelines

### Mash Process

1. Standard Single Infusion

- Strike temp: 168°F for target 152°F
- Water ratio: 1.25-1.5 qt/lb
- Duration: 60 minutes
- Mash-out: 170°F (optional)

2. Step Mash for High Flaked Content

- Protein rest: 122°F (20 min)
- Sacch rest: 152°F (40 min)
- Mash-out: 170°F (10 min)
- Additional water needed

3. Water Adjustments

- Base water profile first
- pH target: 5.2-5.6
- Calcium: 50-150 ppm
- Adjust based on style

### Boil Process

1. Standard Boil

- Duration: 60-90 minutes
- Rolling but not aggressive
- Skim foam first 15 min
- Watch hot break

2. Hop Additions

- First Wort Hops (optional)
- Bittering (60 min)
- Flavor (15-30 min)
- Aroma (0-10 min)

3. Other Additions

- Irish Moss (15 min)
- Yeast Nutrient (10 min)
- Clarity agents if needed

### Fermentation

1. Temperature Control

- Ales: 62-68°F
- Start cool, allow rise
- Avoid temperature swings
- Monitor daily

2. Timeline

- Primary: 7-10 days
- Diacetyl rest: 2 days
- Cold crash: 2-3 days
- Package when stable

## Quality Control

### Raw Material Inspection

1. Malted Barley

- Even color
- No off odors
- Uniform size
- Crisp break
- Proper moisture

2. Flaked Grains

- No clumping
- Fresh smell
- Proper color
- No moisture

3. Storage Requirements

- Cool (<65°F)
- Dry (<13% moisture)
- Dark location
- Air-tight containers
- First in, first out

### Process Control

1. Mash

- pH verification
- Temperature stability
- Conversion test
- Gravity checks

2. Boil

- Rolling boil
- Evaporation rate
- Gravity checks
- Proper break

3. Fermentation

- Temperature log
- Gravity tracking
- Visual inspection
- Taste testing

## Common Problems & Solutions

### Mash Issues

1. Stuck Sparge

- Add rice hulls
- Reduce flaked grains
- Adjust crush
- Proper temperature

2. Poor Efficiency

- Check crush
- Water chemistry
- Mash pH
- Process control

### Fermentation Issues

1. Stuck Fermentation

- Check temperature
- Yeast health
- Oxygen levels
- Nutrient levels

2. Off Flavors

- Temperature control
- Pitch rate
- Sanitation
- Water chemistry
